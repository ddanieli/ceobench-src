03

Customer Parameter Drift

Parameters drift daily: global (all groups), per-group (market trends), per-individual (post-subscription). formula: compound(annual_rate) = (1+rate)^(1/365) − 1

Quality Floor (q_min) — "minimum acceptable quality"

Global drift raises q_min for ALL groups. Competitor events add sudden jumps.

As q_min rises, the agent must invest more in quality or lose customers whose Q falls below the new floor.

Max Budget (c_max) — "most a customer will pay"

Per-group drift varies: some grow (expanding budgets), some shrink (cost-cutting).

Shrinking c_max means the agent must lower prices or risk losing price-sensitive segments entirely.

04

Term Sheet Conditions

Agent can accept conditions that make the deal sweeter for VCs — boosting valuation but creating real downside risk.

🛡️ Anti-Dilution Protection

What it is: A safety net for the VC. If your company's value drops below a floor (e.g., 0.9× original), the VC automatically gets extra shares to compensate.

Valuation boost: up to +5%

Risk: A bad quarter could trigger it, silently diluting the founder's ownership — shrinking your share of future dividends.

📊 Milestone Tranching

What it is: The VC doesn't give all the money upfront. They pay 30–70% now, and the rest only if you hit a revenue target (1.5–3× growth) within a deadline (60–180 days).

Valuation boost: up to +8%

Risk: Miss the milestone → you never get the second tranche. You planned spending around money that never arrives.

💸 Redemption Rights

What it is: After a window (90–365 days), the VC can force you to buy back their shares at 1.0–1.5× what they paid. It's an "eject button" for unhappy investors.

Valuation boost: up to +6%

Risk: If the VC triggers redemption, you must pay out cash — potentially draining reserves right when you need them most.

Max combined boost: +19% (5+8+6). But stacking all three = maximum founder risk. Agent must balance valuation vs. downside.

World Changes — Macro Cycle (PMI)

ISM PMI follows an Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process (mean-reverting random walk). Published monthly with 30-day lag.

PMI affects: VC valuation multiplier · customer willingness to pay · enterprise seat expansion · lead generation

World Changes — Cyclical Patterns (Usage)

Individual subscribers' parameters drift continuously. Group drift (market trends) and individual drift (post-subscription behavior) stack.

New signups sample from group distribution. Only existing subscribers experience individual drift. Group drift affects future arrivals.
